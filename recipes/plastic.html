The Yummiest Plastic Recipe Around

Description

This recipe is near and dear to me. Passed down through the generations, this recipe has been a staple in my family's household and a personal favorite.

I still remember those sweet summer nights sitting on my grammy's porch, watching the sunset. She would come out with a fresh, steaming hot bowl of the stuff and I would go to town on it everytime. No one made the stuff like grammy did.

Ingredients

  • ...Milk...?
  • Vinegar
  • Large pot
  • Larger pot
  • Spoon
  • The finest colander/strainer that money can buy
  • Stove, naturally
  • Paper towels. So. Many. Paper towels.

Directions

  1. Depending on how much plastic you wish to enjoy, portion out 1 TBSP of vinegar for every cup of milk.
  2. Bring milk to a simmer in the large pot, stirring occasionally.
  3. Once milk is simmering, turn off the heat, pour in the vinegar, and stir.
  4. Slowly pour the delectable, chunky milk through the colander and into the larger pot
  5. Sop up the excess liquid with a paper towel
  6. Let cool and bone apple teeth!
